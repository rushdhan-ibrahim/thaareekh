## CRITICAL MISSING INTER-DYNASTY CONNECTIONS

### 1. HILAALY → HURAAGEY (the connection you flagged)

The Huraagey dynasty is actually a *continuation* of the Hilaaly dynasty through the
Christian Hilaaly kings who lived in Goa. The website explicitly calls the page
"Royal House of Hilaaly-Huraa" and treats them as one continuous family.

**The lineage chain (from kings list entry #80):**
Sultan Hassan Izzuddine (#80, founder of Huraagey, r.1759-1766) was:
  → son of Amina Dio (daughter of Mohamed Kateeb of Muli)
    AND Huraa Mohamed Faamuladeyri Thakurufan
      → son of Huraa Hussain Daharadha Thakurufan
        → son of Huraa Dom Maraduru Fandiaiy Thakurufan
          → son of Dom Luis de Sousa
            → son of Donna Ines
              → daughter of King Joao (#66, Hilaaly) and Donna Francisca Vasconcellos

**Missing edge:** Joao (#66, Hilaaly) is the patrilineal ancestor of Hassan Izzuddine (#80, Huraagey)
through 5 intermediate generations. This is the KEY inter-dynasty bridge.

Also: King Philippe (#67) was "Son of #66 and Donna Francisca Vasconcellos" — same parents as
Donna Ines. So Philippe (#67) and Donna Ines were siblings, and Donna Ines → Dom Luis →
Dom Maraduru → Hussain Daharadha → Mohamed Faamuladeyri → Hassan Izzuddine (#80).

**Intermediate non-sovereign persons to potentially add:**

- Mohamed Faamuladeyri Thakurufan (son of Hussain Daharadha, father of #80)

### 2. HILAALY → UTHEEMU CONNECTION

Mohamed Thakurufan (Kateeb of Utheem, the national hero, de facto ruler ~1573-1585) was from
the **Hilaaly extended family**. The website states explicitly:
- "Son of Hussain Thakurufan son of Kalhu Ali Thakurufan of Utheem who was a member of the
  Hilaaly extended family."
- The Utheem page adds: "Kalu Hassan Kaleyge was of Hilaaly descent" and was a contemporary
  of Sultan Abubakur I (#40, Hilaaly).
- "Those who fought to succeed the Christian Hilaalys to the throne of the Maldives were indeed
  Hilaalys themselves."
- "so ended the Utheem Dynasty, very likely a branch of the Hilaaly Dynasty."

**Kalu Thukkalaa / Mohamed Imaduddine I (#68, first legally proclaimed Utheem sultan):**
His mother was Aminah Mava Kilege → daughter of Mariyam Kambaadhi Kilege → daughter of
Ali Thakurufan Kateeb of Maarandu → son of Hussain Thakurufan Kateeb of Utheem.
This makes #68 connected to Mohamed Thakurufan's family through the same patriarchal Utheem line.

Also: #68's mother's grandmother was Ali Thakurufan's daughter, and Ali Thakurufan was the
elder half-brother of Mohamed Thakurufan.

**Missing connection type:** kin/lineage link from Hilaaly dynasty to Mohamed Thakurufan
and therefore to the Utheemu dynasty.

### 3. LUNAR → HILAALY CONNECTION

The founders of the Hilaaly dynasty (the twins Hassan #30 and Hussain #32) had maternal
connections to the Lunar Dynasty:
- Their mother **Golaavahi Kambulo** was "very likely a member of the then recently displaced
  Lunar Dynasty" (per the Hilaaly page).
- Their father **Hilaaly Kalo** (aka Kulhiveri Hilaal Kaiulhanna Kaloge) was son of
  **Muslim Abbas of Hilaal** (apparently of Yemeni descent).
- The uncle **Nisf Handeygirin** (Maabandeyri Yoosuf Handeygirin) was brother of Hilaaly Kalo
  and also son of Muslim Abbas of Hilaal.

The last Lunar sovereign was Raadhafathi (#25) who was deposed by her husband.
Then Mohamed of Maakurathu (#26) reigned, then his daughter Dhaain (#27), then Abdullah (#28),
then Osman of Fehendu (#29), and THEN Hassan (#30, first Hilaaly) took the throne.

**Golaavahi Kambulo is believed to be Lunar dynasty** → making Hassan (#30) and Hussain (#32)
half-Lunar, half-foreign (Yemeni/Hilaali).

**Missing edge:** Inferred kin/lineage connection between the late Lunar dynasty and the
Hilaaly founders through Golaavahi Kambulo.

### 4. DIYAMIGILI → HURAAGEY CONNECTION

Sultan Mohamed Ghiyathuddine (#81, Diyamigili) was deposed while on Haj. He was "Son of #78
(Ibrahim Iskander, Diyamigili) and Aysha Dio."

His cousin Sultan Mohamed Shamsuddine (#82, Huraagey) replaced him. #82 was "Son of Zuleikha
Dio and Hussain Bodu Dorhimeyna Kilege uncle of #80."

This means Hussain Bodu Dorhimeyna Kilege was both:
- Father of #82 (Huraagey)
- Uncle of #80 (Hassan Izzuddine, Huraagey founder)

The connection between Diyamigili's fall and Huraagey's rise: The Malabar occupation (1752-1757)
ended the Diyamigili dynasty. The person who liberated Malé was "Muleegey Dom Hassan Maniku,
a direct descendant of the penultimate Christian King Joao" — i.e., a Hilaaly descendant, who
then installed the Huraagey (=Hilaaly restored) dynasty.

### 5. HILAALY-ERA INTER-DYNASTIC MARRIAGES (non-sovereign spouses)

**Ali VI (#63) marriage to Hilaaly princess:**
"He was married to Princess Aysha Rani Kilege, aunt of #61/#64/#65 and daughter of #51/#54/#58"
→ Ali (#63, classified Unknown) married a daughter of Kalu Mohamed (#54, Hilaaly)
→ This makes #63 connected to the Hilaaly dynasty by marriage

**Mohamed Thakurufan's marriage:**
He married Sitti Maavaa Rani Kilege, daughter of Ali VI (#63) and Kambaa Aysha Rani Kilege.
→ Kambaa Aysha Rani Kilege was widow of Ali VI and daughter of Kalu Mohamed (#58) + Burecca.

## KEY NARRATIVES FROM THE SOURCE

### The Hilaaly Origin Story
In ~1388, a poor couple from Hulhulé — Hilaaly Kalo (of Yemeni descent) and his wife
Golaavahi Kambulo (probably displaced Lunar Dynasty nobility, described as an astrologer) —
moved to Malé. They had twin sons, Hassan and Hussain. The Prime Minister Nisf Handeygirin
(actually Hilaaly Kalo's own brother!) tried to prevent them settling because he foresaw the
twins would depose Sultan Osman. Hassan found a treasure ship, used it to gain favour, then
deposed Osman while the sultan was away fishing. Thus began the Hilaaly Dynasty in 1388.

### The Hilaaly-Huraagey Continuity
The website treats Hilaaly and Huraagey as ONE dynasty ("Royal House of Hilaaly-Huraa").
When Hassan IX converted to Christianity (becoming Dom Manoel, ~1552), he went to Goa.
His descendants — through his grandson Joao, great-grandson Philippe, and the Donna Ines
line — maintained the royal claim. Meanwhile Mohamed Thakurufan (himself Hilaaly by blood)
ruled as regent/de facto sultan. Eventually the Malabar occupation ended the Diyamigili
interlude, and "Muleegey Dom Hassan Maniku, a direct descendant of the penultimate Christian
King Joao" liberated Malé, installing the Huraagey dynasty — which was simply the Hilaaly
line restored through the Goa descendants.

### The Utheem as Hilaaly Branch
"So ended the Utheem Dynasty, very likely a branch of the Hilaaly Dynasty."
Mohamed Thakurufan's grandfather Kalu Hassan Kaleyge "was of Hilaaly descent."
The Utheem Thakurufans were "regarded by many as the rightful heirs to the throne, when the
line of King Siri Dhirikusa Loka (Hassan IX later Manoel) became Christians."

### Princess Recca and Burecca
One of the most dramatic stories: Recca, daughter of Sultan Aboobakuru (#40), married
the shipwright Mohamed Farhana Kalo. She was poisoned by her stepmother but survived in a
coma, secretly nursed back to health. She had three children: Burecca, Reccy, and Ali.
Burecca was a martial arts expert who traveled to Aceh (Sumatra), returned, and killed her
own brother Ali (#57) in a sword duel to seize the throne for her husband Kalu Mohamed.
