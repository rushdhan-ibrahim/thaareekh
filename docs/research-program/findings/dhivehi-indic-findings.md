## 1. Regnal Epithet Etymology (Resolved)

### Key Reference Sources
- **Hassan Ahmed Maniku**, *A Concise Etymological Vocabulary of Dhivehi Language* (Colombo: Royal Asiatic Society of Sri Lanka, 2000). Available via DSAL, University of Chicago. Maniku documents that "the etymology of the vast majority of Dhivehi words could be traced to Sinhala, Sanskrit and Pali," forming the oldest linguistic strata predating Islam. He documents systematic sound shifts: p-sounds becoming f-sounds post-conversion; s/h interchange; dh-consonants transforming into y-sounds.
- **Wilhelm Geiger**, *Maldivian Linguistic Studies* (early 20th c.). Geiger assumes Maldivian is a dialectal offspring of Sinhalese, concluding it split from Sinhalese not earlier than the 10th century CE. Both languages constitute the "Insular Indo-Aryan" subgroup, descended from Elu Prakrit of ancient/medieval Sri Lanka.
- **Sonja Fritz**, *The Dhivehi Language: A Descriptive and Historical Grammar of Maldivian and Its Dialects*, 2 vols (2002). The fullest modern grammar; documents the relationship between Dhivehi and Sinhalese as "the most important objective when the history of the language is concerned."
- **RoyalArk Maldives Glossary** (royalark.net/Maldives/maldives.htm) provides definitions for Radun, Rehendi, Maha, Didi, Kilege.
- **Maldives Royal Family website** (maldivesroyalfamily.com) provides context on gong ceremony regnal names.

### General Phonological Pathway: Sanskrit/Pali --> Sinhala --> Dhivehi
Key sound changes documented:
- Sanskrit /j/ --> Dhivehi /d/ (via intermediate /dz/). E.g., Sanskrit yatra --> Dhivehi daturu.
- Sanskrit /p/ --> Dhivehi /f/ (post-17th century). E.g., pas (five) --> fas.
- Sanskrit cluster simplification: consonant clusters reduced.
- Vowel changes: long vowels often preserved or lengthened further; ee for i.
- Retroflex sounds retained uniquely in Dhivehi where Sinhala merged them.

### 1.1 Keerithi -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: kirti / kirti (kirti, "fame, glory, renown")
- **Dhivehi form**: Keerithi (with vowel lengthening ee for i, aspirated th)
- **Meaning**: Fame, glory, renown. Literally "the thing that speaks of or glorifies one." Also used to mean "any work which renders the constructor of it famous; a meritorious work."
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit kirti --> Prakrit kitti --> Sinhala kirthi --> Dhivehi Keerithi (characteristic Dhivehi vowel lengthening of short i to ee)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Used as a default regnal name component when no coronation ceremony was performed. "Keerithi Maha Radun" for kings and "Keerithi Maha Rehendi" for queens who did not have a gong ceremony. Appears 17 times in the dataset.
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib Sanskrit definitions; RoyalArk Maldives glossary; MRF kings list; Maniku's DSAL dictionary (general framework).
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.2 Maha -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: maha (maha, "great, mighty")
- **Dhivehi form**: Maha (unchanged)
- **Meaning**: Great. Standard Indo-Aryan honorific prefix.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit maha --> Pali maha --> Sinhala maha --> Dhivehi Maha (no change; the word is pan-Indo-Aryan)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears as a standard honorific element, always paired with Radun (for kings) or Rehendi (for queens). "Maha Radun" = "great king" (cf. maharaja). Appears 15 times in dataset.
- **Attestation**: RoyalArk glossary explicitly defines it as "great"; pan-Indic cognate (maharaja, mahatma, etc.).
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.3 Radun -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: rajan (rajan, "king, ruler, sovereign")
- **Dhivehi form**: Radun (with characteristic Insular Indo-Aryan consonant shift j-->d and terminal -un from oblique case stem)
- **Meaning**: King, ruler, sovereign.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit rajan --> Prakrit raja --> Sinhala rajun/radun (with voicing change j-->d in Insular Indo-Aryan) --> Dhivehi Radun. The j-->d shift is a known Insular Indo-Aryan development (same shift seen in yatra-->daturu). The -n terminal mirrors the Sanskrit nominative stem.
- **Usage in regnal names**: Male sovereign designation. "Maha Radun" = "Great King." "Keerithi Maha Radun" = "Glorious Great King." The pre-Islamic and continuing Dhivehi title for the ruling monarch. Appears 14 times.
- **Attestation**: RoyalArk glossary: "Radun: raja, ruler, king." H.C.P. Bell records "Maha Radun" as the old Dhivehi title retained alongside Sultan.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.4 Bavana -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: bhavana (bhavana, "producing, manifesting; dwelling, abode; thought, contemplation")
- **Dhivehi form**: Bavana (with characteristic devoicing of bh-->b and retention of vowel pattern)
- **Meaning**: In royal regnal context, likely "abode/dwelling" (i.e., "shelter/refuge of [the realm]") or "producer/creator" (of prosperity). Both meanings are attested in Sanskrit.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit bhavana --> Prakrit bhavana --> Sinhala bhavana --> Dhivehi Bavana (aspiration loss bh-->b is regular in Dhivehi)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 13 regnal names. Forms compounds like "Bavana Abarana" (P3, "Ornament of the Abode/Realm"), "Bavana Sooja" (P21), "Sundhura Bavana" (P17, "Beautiful Abode"), "Raadha Bavana" (P38).
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib Sanskrit definitions; Maniku's dictionary framework for bh-->b deaspirated forms.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.5 Loka -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: loka (loka, "world, realm, people, universe")
- **Dhivehi form**: Loka (unchanged from Sanskrit/Pali)
- **Meaning**: World, realm, people. In Buddhist cosmology: a plane of existence. In Pali used for "all that can be known or experienced."
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit loka --> Pali loka --> Sinhala loka --> Dhivehi Loka (no change needed; already simple CV-CV structure)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 13 regnal names. "Loka Suvara" (P10, "Gold of the World"), "Loka Abarana" (P18, "Ornament of the World"), "Loka Aadheeththa" (P19, "Sun of the World"), "Loka Veeru" (P32, "Hero of the World").
- **Attestation**: Wikipedia on Loka; WisdomLib (53 definitions); Britannica.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.6 Abarana -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: abharana (abharana, "ornament, decoration, jewel")
- **Dhivehi form**: Abarana (with deaspiration of bh-->b and vowel simplification)
- **Meaning**: Ornament, decoration. In Sanskrit literary tradition, one of the four types of alankara (decoration). Etymology: a-bhr + ana (from root bhr, "to fill/nourish" with prefix a).
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit abharana --> Prakrit abarana --> Sinhala abarana --> Dhivehi Abarana (aspiration loss bh-->b is regular)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 10 regnal names. "Bavana Abarana" (P3, "Ornament of the Abode"), "Dhagathaa Abarana" (P6), "Loka Abarana" (P18, "Ornament of the World"), "Suvama Abarana" (P25, "Golden Ornament"), "Raadha Abarana" (P20, "Ornament of Prosperity").
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib (35 definitions); SanskritWisdom ornament listing.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.7 Kula -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: kula (kula, "family, lineage, clan, race, noble house")
- **Dhivehi form**: Kula (unchanged)
- **Meaning**: Family, lineage, noble house. In Sanskrit: "possessing form/nature." Primary usage in royal context: "of [noble] lineage."
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit kula --> Pali kula --> Sinhala kula --> Dhivehi Kula (no change)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 10 regnal names, predominantly in later dynasties (Utheemu, Dhiyamigili, Huraagey). "Kula Sundhura Katthiri Bavana" (P68), "Kula Ran Meeba Katthiri" (P69), "Kula Ran Mani" (P73), "Kula Ran Meeba Audha" (P80), "Kula Ran Muiy" (P74).
- **Attestation**: Standard Sanskrit dictionaries; pan-Indic usage in royal nomenclature.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.8 Sundhura -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: sundara (sundara, "beautiful, handsome, pleasing")
- **Dhivehi form**: Sundhura (with characteristic Dhivehi consonant shift nd-->ndh and vowel backing a-->u in final syllable)
- **Meaning**: Beautiful, handsome, pleasing.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit sundara --> Prakrit sundara --> Sinhala sundara --> Dhivehi Sundhura (the nd-->ndh aspiration and terminal -a --> -u are characteristic Insular Indo-Aryan developments; the r-metathesis places r before final vowel)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 9 regnal names. "Sundhura Bavana" (P17, "Beautiful Abode"), "Sundhura Abarana" (P26, "Beautiful Ornament"), "Sundhura" alone (P29).
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib (32 definitions for sundara); Maniku's general framework.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.9 Raadha -- PARTIALLY RESOLVED (E2)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: radha (radha, "prosperity, success, perfection, wealth")
- **Dhivehi form**: Raadha (with vowel lengthening aa and aspiration dh)
- **Meaning**: Prosperity, success. In Sanskrit, radha means "success, prosperity" (cf. name Anuradha = "following prosperity").
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit radha --> Prakrit radha --> Sinhala radha --> Dhivehi Raadha (vowel lengthening a-->aa is characteristic of Dhivehi)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 7 regnal names. "Raadha Suvara" (P9, "Golden Prosperity"), "Raadha Abarana" (P20, "Ornament of Prosperity"), "Raadha Veeru" (P41, "Hero of Prosperity"), "Raadha Bavana" (P38).
- **Note**: Could also connect to Sanskrit rajya ("kingdom/rule") through Prakrit intermediary, but radha ("prosperity") is the more direct and semantically appropriate derivation.
- **Attestation**: Wikipedia on Radha; WisdomLib.
- **Confidence**: E2 (moderate -- the radha --> Raadha pathway is straightforward but the exact semantic intention in Maldivian regnal context is unconfirmed by direct Dhivehi lexicographic source)

### 1.10 Veeru -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: vira (vira, "hero, brave, heroic, mighty")
- **Dhivehi form**: Veeru (with vowel lengthening and terminal -u)
- **Meaning**: Hero, brave one, mighty warrior.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit vira --> Pali vira --> Sinhala viru --> Dhivehi Veeru (characteristic terminal -u and vowel lengthening ee)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 7 regnal names. "Dhammaru Veeru" (P31, "Righteous Hero"), "Loka Veeru" (P32, "Hero of the World"), "Veeru Abarana" (P33, "Ornament of the Hero"), "Raadha Veeru" (P41, "Hero of Prosperity").
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib (60 definitions for vira); standard Sanskrit-Pali dictionaries.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.11 Suvara -- PARTIALLY RESOLVED (E2)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: suvarna (suvarna, "gold, golden; of good colour")
- **Dhivehi form**: Suvara (with consonant simplification rn-->r and terminal vowel adjustment)
- **Meaning**: Gold, golden. Etymology: su- ("good") + varna ("colour") = "of good colour." In Pali: suvanna, meaning "of good colour, good, favoured, beautiful."
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit suvarna --> Pali suvanna --> Sinhala suvarna/suvana --> Dhivehi Suvara (cluster simplification rn-->r is regular in Insular Indo-Aryan; loss of nasal n before terminal vowel)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 6 regnal names. "Dhagathaa Suvara" (P7), "Raadha Suvara" (P9, "Golden Prosperity"), "Loka Suvara" (P10, "Gold of the World"), "Madheenee Suvara" (P12), "Areedha Suvara" (P13, P14).
- **Note**: Alternatively could connect to svara ("sound, voice, tone") but suvarna ("gold") is far more common in royal epithet tradition and semantically appropriate.
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib (46 definitions for suvarna); Wiktionary Sanskrit.
- **Confidence**: E2 (moderate -- suvarna-->Suvara is plausible but the nasal loss needs direct Dhivehi phonological attestation)

### 1.12 Dhammaru -- PARTIALLY RESOLVED (E2)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: dharma/dhamma (dharma/dhamma, "righteousness, law, duty, virtue, cosmic order")
- **Dhivehi form**: Dhammaru (with gemination mm from Pali dhamma + terminal -ru suffix)
- **Meaning**: Righteous, virtuous. The -ru suffix may be an agentive/adjectival suffix meaning "one who embodies dharma."
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit dharma --> Pali dhamma (regular r-loss and gemination: rm-->mm) --> Dhivehi Dhammaru (retaining Pali form dhamma- + Dhivehi adjectival suffix -ru). The -ru suffix parallels other Dhivehi formations.
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 5 regnal names. "Dhammaru Naaja" (P4), "Dhammaru Veeru" (P31, "Righteous Hero"), "Dhammaru Aadheeththa" (P23), "Dhammaru Loaka" (P37), "Dhammaru Bavana" (P51).
- **Note**: The -ru terminal is distinctive and not directly from Sanskrit/Pali. It may be a Dhivehi agentive suffix or honorific particle. Compare Sinhala -ru in compound formations.
- **Attestation**: Pali Text Society dictionary for dhamma; general Sanskrit-Pali phonological rules.
- **Confidence**: E2 (moderate -- dhamma base is certain, but the -ru suffix formation pathway needs further attestation)

### 1.13 Ran -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: hiranya (hiranya, "gold, golden") via Sinhala ran
- **Dhivehi form**: Ran
- **Meaning**: Gold, golden.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit hiranya --> Prakrit hirana --> Sinhala ran (with extreme consonant reduction and h-loss characteristic of Sinhala) --> Dhivehi ran (borrowed from Sinhala form)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 5 compound names. "Kula Ran Meeba Katthiri" (P69), "Kula Ran Mani" (P73), "Kula Ran Muiy" (P74), "Kula Ran Meeba Audha" (P80).
- **Distinct from**: ranna ("jewel") which derives from Sanskrit ratna.
- **Attestation**: Maniku's dictionary general framework; Sinhala ran = gold is well-established.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.14 Katthiri -- PARTIALLY RESOLVED (E3)
- **Suspected origin**: Multiple possibilities:
  - (a) Arabic kathir (kathir, "abundant, plentiful") -- would represent an Arabic loanword layer in regnal naming post-Islamization
  - (b) Sanskrit kshatriya (ksatriya, "warrior, ruling class") --> Prakrit khattiya --> Dhivehi Katthiri (with regular Prakrit simplification of ksh-->kh-->k and Dhivehi gemination tt)
- **Dhivehi form**: Katthiri
- **Meaning**: If Arabic: "abundant, plentiful." If Sanskrit: "of the warrior/ruling class."
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 4 regnal names. "Audha Siyaaaka Katthiri" (P63), "Kula Sundhura Katthiri Bavana" (P68, Utheemu dynasty), "Kula Ran Meeba Katthiri" (P69), "Kula Sundhura Katthiri" (P91).
- **Note**: The predominance in Utheemu and later dynasty names (post-Islamization) slightly favors the Arabic origin hypothesis. However, the Prakrit ksatriya pathway is phonologically plausible and semantically apt for royal nomenclature.
- **Attestation**: Standard Arabic dictionaries for kathir; Sanskrit ksatriya via Pali khattiya is well-documented.
- **Confidence**: E3 (provisional -- both etymologies are plausible; needs Maniku or Fritz to adjudicate)

### 1.15 Siyaaaka -- PARTIALLY RESOLVED (E3)
- **Suspected origin**: Possibly Sanskrit svaka (svaka, "one's own") or saka ("own, personal, one's own power")
- **Dhivehi form**: Siyaaaka (with vowel lengthening and y-insertion characteristic of Dhivehi)
- **Meaning**: Possibly "sovereign, self-ruling, autonomous" (from svaka = "one's own")
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit svaka --> Prakrit siyaka --> Dhivehi Siyaaaka (s-retention, v-->iy is a known Insular Indo-Aryan development; vowel lengthening aa)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 4 regnal names. "Audha Siyaaaka Katthiri" (P63), "Kula Sundhura Siyaaaka" (P77, Dhiyamigili dynasty), "Kula Sudha Ira Siyaaaka" (P86, P94, Huraagey dynasty).
- **Attestation**: Speculative; based on phonological reconstruction.
- **Confidence**: E3 (provisional -- plausible but not confirmed by any direct source)

### 1.16 Aadheeththa -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: aditya (aditya, "sun; solar deity; descendant of Aditi")
- **Dhivehi form**: Aadheeththa (with vowel lengthening aa, aspiration dh for d, gemination tth, aspiration th for t)
- **Meaning**: Sun. As a royal epithet: "sun-like, radiant, solar."
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit aditya --> Pali adicca --> Sinhala aditya --> Dhivehi Aadheeththa (characteristic Dhivehi transformations: vowel lengthening a-->aa, retroflex d-->dh, t-->tth with aspiration and gemination)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 3 regnal names. "Loka Aadheeththa" (P19, "Sun of the World"), "Dhammaru Aadheeththa" (P23, "Sun of Righteousness"). Also in P2's compound "Bavanaadheeththa" (= Bavana + Aadheeththa, "Sun of the Abode").
- **Attestation**: Standard Sanskrit dictionaries; parallels solar epithets across Indo-Aryan royal traditions.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.17 Aanandha -- RESOLVED (E1)
- **Sanskrit/Pali original**: ananda (ananda, "bliss, joy, happiness")
- **Dhivehi form**: Aanandha (with vowel lengthening aa and aspiration dh)
- **Meaning**: Bliss, joy, happiness. In Hindu philosophy, one of three qualities of ultimate reality (sat-chit-ananda). In Pali, especially associated with the Buddha's disciple Ananda.
- **Phonological pathway**: Sanskrit ananda --> Pali ananda --> Sinhala ananda --> Dhivehi Aanandha (vowel lengthening a-->aa, aspiration of d-->dh are regular Dhivehi developments)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 3 regnal names. "Loka Aananadha" (P39, "Bliss of the World"), "Raadha Aanandha" (P47, "Bliss of Prosperity"), "Veeru Aanandha" (P52, "Bliss of the Hero").
- **Attestation**: WisdomLib (67 definitions); standard Pali dictionaries; Wikipedia.
- **Confidence**: E1 (strong)

### 1.18 Fennaadheeththa -- PARTIALLY RESOLVED (E2)
- **Suspected origin**: Compound of unknown first element "Fenna-" + Aadheeththa (Sanskrit aditya, "sun")
- **Dhivehi form**: Fennaadheeththa
- **Meaning**: "[?]-Sun." The Aadheeththa component is clear (= sun). "Fenna" may relate to Sinhala pena/penna ("visible, appearing, shining") with the characteristic p-->f shift in Dhivehi (documented post-17th century, but possibly earlier in some contexts).
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears once, P5. Also attested on a Loamaafaanu copper plate from AD 1194: "Siri Fennaadheettha Mahaa Radun."
- **Attestation**: Loamaafaanu copper plate evidence; Aadheeththa component is E1.
- **Confidence**: E2 for the compound (Aadheeththa half is E1, Fenna- half is E3)

### 1.19 Dhagathaa -- UNRESOLVED (E4)
- **Suspected origin**: Unknown. Possibly related to Sanskrit daksha ("able, competent, skillful") or dagdha ("burned, tested by fire") but neither is phonologically convincing.
- **Dhivehi form**: Dhagathaa
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 2 regnal names. "Dhagathaa Abarana" (P6), "Dhagathaa Suvara" (P7).
- **Note**: Both P6 and P7 are siblings (Lunar dynasty, early 13th c.), suggesting this was a lineage-specific regnal element.

### 1.20 Sooja -- UNRESOLVED (E4)
- **Suspected origin**: Possibly Sanskrit suja ("well-born") or suka/sukha ("happiness, comfort") or suca ("pure"). None is phonologically exact.
- **Dhivehi form**: Sooja (with vowel lengthening oo)
- **Usage in regnal names**: Appears in 2 regnal names. "Bavana Sooja" (P21, P40).
- **Confidence**: E4 (unresolved)

## 2. Raadhavalhi Findings

### 2.1 Manuscript Tradition Overview
The Raadhavalhi was written in 1757 using both Dhives Akuru and Thaana scripts. It is one of three primary native Maldivian historical sources:
1. **Tarikh-i-Islam Diba al-Mahal** (Hasan Taj al-Din, d. 1727) -- Arabic chronicle covering 670 years
2. **Loamaafaanu copper plates** -- inscription-based, oldest dating to 12th century
3. **Raadhavalhi** -- king list in Dhivehi, compiled 1757

The Raadhavalhi uses the eveyla akuru script (one of the earliest Dhivehi writing systems) alongside Thaana. It was registered in the project as SRC-SARUNA-RAADHAVALHI-1985 (the 1985 scholarly publication).

### 2.2 King List Comparison with Project Data
The Raadhavalhi begins with "Mahaabarana Mahaaradhun" (also known as Koimala Kalo) -- this matches the project's P1 (Koimala, rg: "Maanaabarana"). The variant spellings "Mahaabarana" vs. project's "Maanaabarana" represent a documented variant.

The Loamaafaanu copper plate from AD 1194 records the reign of "Siri Fennaadheettha Mahaa Radun" -- this corresponds to P5 (Dhinei, rg: "Fennaadheeththa"), confirming the regnal name independently of the chronicle tradition.

### 2.3 Variant Names Found
- P1: "Mahaabarana Mahaaradhun" (Raadhavalhi) vs. "Maanaabarana" (project) -- the "Mahaa" vs. "Maana" prefix difference may reflect scribal variation or a distinct recension.
- The Loamaafaanu version gives regnal names with the "Siri" prefix (e.g., "Siri Fennaadheettha") which is absent from the project's regnal names. "Siri" corresponds to Sanskrit shri ("holy, auspicious") and was likely an honorific prefix dropped in later tradition.

### 2.4 Genealogical Claims
The Raadhavalhi's genealogical structure provides lineage framing for the Lunar dynasty rulers. Its use in conjunction with the Tarikh and Loamaafaanu plates allows triangulation of kinship claims. However, detailed comparison requires the full text of the 1985 publication (SRC-SARUNA-RAADHAVALHI-1985).

6. **Eduru Thakurufan** -- son of Kalu Hassan Kaleyge, whose son became Hussain Faamuladeyri Kilege

### 3.4 Thakurufaanu Honorific Etymology
"Thakurufaanu" decomposes as "Thakuru" + "faanu":
- **Thakuru**: From Sanskrit/Prakrit thakkura (village headman, lord), borrowed into Hindi as thakur (master, chief, lord). Ultimately may derive from the Tukharistan region of Central Asia (a borrowed word into Sanskrit, per scholarly opinion). Meaning: "lord, master."
- **Faanu/faan**: A Dhivehi honorific suffix showing reverence, used in addressing respected persons. Cf. Eesaagefaanu (Jesus), Rasgefaanu (sovereign). The -faanu particle functions as a respectful particle similar to Japanese -sama.
- **Combined meaning**: "Revered lord/master." The title was a middle-class rank cognomen in the MRF titles hierarchy, below Manikfan but above Kaleyfan.

## 4. Mahavamsa/Sinhalese Findings

### 4.1 "Diva" References
The Maldives was known as "Dheeva Mahal" or "Diva Mahal" in ancient references. The Arabic designation "Mahaldibu" is connected to the Mahavamsa's "Mahiladipa" (Tamil: "Mahal" = "great"; Sanskrit: "dvipa" = "island"). The Mahavamsa (c. 300 BCE) documents individuals from Sri Lanka relocating to the Maldives.

The Mahavamsa records that in 500 BCE, people traveled from Sinhapura (NE India) to both Sri Lanka and the Maldives -- one of the ships arrived in Sri Lanka while the other arrived in the Maldives (though this specific claim is legendary rather than historical).

### 4.2 Koimala Legend Analysis
According to the chronicle tradition (Tarikh and Raadhavalhi), Koimala was a Sinhalese prince of royal lineage from Ceylon who married the daughter of the King of Lanka. Departing with his wife, he reached Resgatimu (Rasgetimu Island) in Northern Maalhosmadulhu Atoll. The Buddhist population invited them to remain, and Koimala was crowned king. Vessels were subsequently dispatched to Lanka to bring "people of the Lion Race" (Sinhalese), and thus the Maldives came to be colonized.

**Project alignment**: P1 (Koimala, dy: "Lunar", pb: "Ceylon (traditionally stated)") matches this narrative. His regnal name "Maanaabarana" / "Mahaabarana" is pure Sanskrit (maha + abharana = "great ornament"), consistent with a Sinhalese Buddhist naming tradition.

### 4.3 Pre-Islamic Connections
Archaeological evidence documented by H.C.P. Bell confirms Buddhist and Hindu material culture in the Maldives before the 1153 CE Islamic conversion:
- Stupas, finials, capitals, carved stones, images found on Gan and Fua Mulaku
- The first Loamaafaanu copper plates were discovered in 1922 during Bell's expedition
- Archaeological inscriptions found in the Maldives are "nearly identical to those found in Sri Lanka"
- The former national hospital bore the name "Dharumavantha" -- a name of "distinctly Sinhala origin"

The Buddhist-Islamic transition preserved vocabulary and naming conventions. P2 (Dhovemi) "reigned as Buddhist until 1153 conversion to Islam" -- his regnal name "Bavanaadheeththa" (Bavana + Aadheeththa = "Sun of the Abode") is entirely Sanskrit/Pali in composition, despite being the first Muslim ruler.

## 5. South Indian Findings

### 5.1 Trade/Political Connections
**Chola Conquest**: Rajaraja Chola I (r. 985-1014) conquered the Maldives as part of his vast maritime empire. By 1014, the Chola empire controlled Lakshadweep and the Maldives. Rajendra Chola I continued this dominion. The Chola occupation predates the Koimala era (1117) by approximately a century, and the political disruption may have created the conditions for Koimala's establishment of a new ruling house.

**Mappila Muslim Trade**: The Cannanore (Kannur) Mappila Muslim merchants dominated the Indian Ocean trade routes including routes to Canara, Malabar, Ceylon, Maldives, and the Coromandel Coast. Their ships connected the Maldives to the broader Indian Ocean commercial network. The coir and cowrie trade was economically significant.

### 5.2 Marriage Alliances
No direct South Indian marriage alliances with Maldivian royalty were documented in the sources reviewed. However, the Mappila Muslim trade networks and the presence of Arab merchants (noted as marrying into local families) suggest commercial-matrimonial connections that would require further research in South Indian port city records (Calicut, Cannanore, Cochin).

## 6. Female Title Forms

### 6.1 Rehendhi/Rani Analysis
Two distinct female royal titles with different functions:

**Rehendhi** (also Rehendi):
- Meaning: Regnant female ruler (a woman who rules in her own right)
- Post-Islamic equivalent: Sultana
- Typical compound: "Katthiri Maha Rehendi" or "Keerithi Maha Rehendi"
- Etymology: Likely derived from Sinhala/Prakrit form related to Sanskrit rajni (rajni, "queen, female ruler"), with characteristic r-->r retention, j-->h or j-loss, and -ni-->-ndi terminal shift. The development rajni --> rehendi would follow Insular Indo-Aryan patterns (vowel changes, nasal/dental cluster development). However, this derivation is not explicitly attested in available sources.
- **Confidence**: E2 (the derivation is phonologically plausible but not directly confirmed)

**Rani** (also Rani Kilege):
- Meaning: Queen consort (the formally designated wife of the ruling sovereign)
- Sanskrit origin: rajni (rajni, "queen, princess") -- this is E1 confirmed
- Distinction from Rehendi: Rani denotes consort status, not regnant authority. When Burecca of the Maldives took the title "Rani" instead of "Rehendi," it signified she was queen consort rather than ruling monarch.

**Key distinction**: Rehendi = queen regnant; Rani = queen consort. This parallels the Radun/Raja distinction where Radun is the specifically Dhivehi form for the ruling sovereign.

### 6.2 Kabaidhi/Kambaadi
From the MRF titles page, Kambaadi Kilege is one of the female Kilege titles (alongside Rani, Maavaa, and Maanayaa). The term appears in genealogical records: "Maryam Kambaadi Kilege" (daughter of Ali Thakurufan). Etymology unresolved -- possibly from a Dhivehi compound; requires Maniku dictionary attestation.
- **Confidence**: E4 (unresolved)

### 6.3 Maava Kilege
"Maava Kilege" appears frequently in the sovereign notes: "son of Rekeihiriya Maava Kilege" (P4), "son of Fathahiriya Maava Kilege" (P5), "son of Hiriya Maava Kilege" (P9), "daughter of Aidage Maava Kilege" (P11, P12).

The compound decomposes as:
- **Maava**: Possibly from Sanskrit maha (maha, "great") with Dhivehi v-insertion, or from a Dhivehi term meaning "mother/maternal." The recurrent use in maternal attribution ("son of X Maava Kilege") suggests a maternal/matrilineal connotation.
- **Kilege**: The highest non-sovereign title in the Maldivian court hierarchy, awarded by the sultan/sultana. From the MRF titles page: "The elevation to the title of kilege raises a person and his or her immediate family to the level of the aristocracy." Female Kilege subtypes: Rani, Maavaa, Kambaadi, Maanayaa.
- **Etymology of Kilege**: Possibly from Sanskrit kulika ("of noble family") or related to Sinhala kulina ("well-born") + ge (Dhivehi suffix for "house/family"). The first element kul- maps to Sanskrit kula ("family, lineage"). The semantic field "of noble house" fits the title's function. However, this derivation is not directly attested.
- **Confidence**: E3 for Kilege etymology; E3 for Maava etymology

### 8.3 Regnal Name "Siri" Prefix
The Loamaafaanu copper plates consistently use a "Siri" prefix before regnal names (e.g., "Siri Fennaadheettha Mahaa Radun") that the project currently omits. This represents Sanskrit shri ("holy, auspicious, blessed") and should be noted as a variant form in the regnal name register, though its omission in the project data is consistent with later chronicle tradition which dropped it.

### 8.4 Koimala Variant Name
The Raadhavalhi gives P1's full regnal style as "Mahaabarana Mahaaradhun" while the project has "Maanaabarana." The variant "Mahaabarana" (= maha + abharana, "great ornament") is semantically clearer and possibly more authentic. "Maanaabarana" may represent a scribal corruption or an alternative etymology (mana + abharana, "mind's ornament"). This should be recorded as a contested variant.
