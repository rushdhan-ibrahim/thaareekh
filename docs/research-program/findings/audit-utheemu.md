# Utheemu + Isdu + Dhiyamigili + Unknown Dynasties Audit

## Summary

This audit covers 30 persons across four dynasty groupings: Utheemu (14 persons), Isdu/Isdu-Linked (4 persons), Dhiyamigili/Dhiyamigili-South (8 persons), and Unknown (4 persons). The period spans roughly 1511 to 1860, covering some of the most dramatic events in Maldivian history: the Portuguese occupation and liberation (1558-1573), the Utheemu golden age (1573-1692), the rapid dynastic turnover of 1692-1704, the Dhiyamigili period (1704-1774), and the Malabar invasion of 1752.

**Key findings:**
- **Duplicate detected:** P105 (Hassan Thakurufan, promoted) and P201 (Hassan Thakurufaanu, base) appear to be the same person -- both described as brother of Mohamed Thakurufaanu (P104). This should be merged.
- **Mohamed Thakurufaanu (P104)** biography is thorough and well-sourced; minor factual enrichments added from Wikipedia.
- **P130 birth year discrepancy:** Dataset says yb:1764, but Wikipedia says born April 16, 1766. The 1766 date aligns with his father P81's death in October 1774 when P130 was "age seven."
- **P72 dynasty classification:** Listed as "Unknown" but research confirms he was a Syrian-born Arab scholar, not from any Maldivian dynasty. "Unknown" is correct.
- **P62 and P63:** Correctly classified as "Unknown" dynasty; P62 was a prime minister who assumed power; P63 married into the Hilaaly family but was not himself a dynastic member.
- **P56:** Correctly classified as "Unknown"; he was an Arab from Mecca, not a member of any Maldivian dynasty.
- All major edges verified against RoyalArk and Wikipedia sources.

### P68 -- Mohamed Imaduddine I (Utheemu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Mohamed Imaduddine I, bearing the Arabic laqab 'Imad al-Din meaning "Pillar of the Faith," was the first sultan of the Utheemu dynasty and the ruler who inaugurated the systematic adoption of Arabic honorific titles in Maldivian sovereignty. His full titulature, Sultan Shuja'at Muhammad Imad ud-din I Bodu Rasgefa'anu, combined the Arabic martial epithet Shuja'at ("Brave") with the Dhivehi royal designation Bodu Rasgefa'anu ("Great Sovereign").

His father was Al-Amir Umar Ma'afai Kilege and his mother Kaba'a Amina Ma'ava Kilege. Through the Utheemu family network he was connected to the liberation hero Mohamed Thakurufaanu (P104), whose nephew's descendants eventually produced the Utheemu sultanic line. Mohamed Imaduddine married three times -- to Aisha Kabafa'anu, Aisha Rani Kilege, and Fatuma Kilage -- and fathered several children, most notably Sultan Ibrahim Iskander I (P69).

His reign from 1632 to his death on October 22, 1648 represented the consolidation of Islamic governance after the tumultuous Portuguese period. The adoption of a formal Arabic laqab signaled a new era of religious legitimacy and closer alignment with the broader Islamic world, replacing the Sanskritic regnal naming conventions that had persisted since the pre-Islamic period.

**Notes**: Existing bio in enrichments is comprehensive and well-sourced. Data record matches: yb:1600, yd:1650, re:1632-1648, dy:Utheemu. No corrections needed.

### P69 -- Iskander Ibrahim I (Utheemu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Ibrahim Iskander I, known by the Hellenistic throne epithet Iskandar meaning "Alexander," was one of the most consequential sultans of the Utheemu dynasty. Born before May 7, 1631, he was the son of Mohamed Imaduddine I (P68) and his first wife Aisha Kabafa'anu. His full Arabic titulature, Sultan al-Ghazi Hajji Ibrahim I Iskandar al-'Izz w'an-Nasr, proclaimed him both a warrior for the faith (al-Ghazi) and a pilgrim to Mecca (Hajji).

His thirty-nine year reign from 1648 to 1687 was marked by both military valor and cultural achievement. He successfully repelled a Portuguese naval attack in 1650, definitively ending the threat of European re-conquest. His greatest architectural legacy was the construction of the Male Friday Mosque, the Hukuru Miskiy, between 1656 and 1658, a masterpiece of coral stone craftsmanship that remains one of the finest examples of medieval Islamic architecture in the Indian Ocean. In 1674 he erected the first minaret in the Maldives, and in 1667 he undertook the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca.

Iskander Ibrahim married twice: first to Lhaimagu Amina Kaba'afa'anu, and then to Maryam Kabafa'anu Rani Kilege. On February 28, 1687, Maryam poisoned her husband, bringing his long reign to an end. She then installed their young son Kuda Mohamed (P70) as sultan and ruled as regent in his name. Maryam's regency ended in 1691 when both she and the child-sultan perished in a gunpowder explosion aboard a vessel near Dunidu Island.

**Notes**: Existing bio comprehensive. Base record has no yb/yd; enrichment supplies born before May 7, 1631. Reign 1648-1687 matches. Son of P68 confirmed. No corrections needed.

### P70 -- Kuda Mohamed (Utheemu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Kuda Mohamed, born on August 24, 1680 at Male, became sultan at the age of seven following the poisoning of his father Ibrahim Iskander I (P69) by his own mother, Maryam Kabafa'anu Rani Kilege, on February 28, 1687. As a child too young to govern, effective power rested entirely with Maryam, who acted as regent.

The regency was marked by the domineering influence of the queen mother, the same woman widely believed to have murdered the previous sultan. In 1691, when the sultan's fleet defeated Malabar pirates from Thiladhummati atoll, a spark from a royal salute caused the gunpowder on the royal vessel to explode near Dunidu Island, killing Maryam and fatally wounding the young sultan. Kuda Mohamed was barely ten years old. The simultaneous death of the child-king and his regent mother brought the direct Utheemu succession line to a catastrophic end.

**Notes**: Base record has yb:1680, no yd. Enrichment confirms details. Re:1687-1691 matches. Son of P69 confirmed. Wikipedia clarifies the explosion was caused by a spark from a royal salute, not malicious intent -- though the circumstances remain debated. No corrections needed to dataset.

### P71 -- Mohamed Mohyedine (Utheemu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Mohamed Mohyedine, bearing the Arabic laqab Muhyi al-Din meaning "Reviver of the Faith," reigned for less than a year from 1691 to 1692. His father was Abu Naibu Hassan Dorhimeyna Kilege (P160), and his mother was Daravandu Kadida Dio (P161). His connection to the Utheemu dynasty was indirect, passing through a collateral line rather than direct patrilineal descent from Mohamed Imaduddine I (P68).

Described in some sources as a religious rigorist (noted as "Fundamentalist Muslim" in base data), Mohyedine briefly married the notorious Maryam Kabafa'anu before divorcing her. He died on February 18, 1692, from illness, having reigned barely eleven months. His death opened the door to Mohamed Shamsuddine (P72), his Syrian-born mentor, who was proclaimed sultan the very next day.

**Notes**: Base record has yb:1655, yd:1700, re:1691-1692. Parents confirmed as P160 (father) and P161 (mother) via promoted edges. The enrichment bio correctly identifies the indirect dynastic connection. No corrections needed.

### P103 -- Hussain Thakurufan (Utheemu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Hussain Thakurufan, known as Katheebu Hussain of Utheemu, was the father of Mohamed Thakurufaanu al-Auzam (P104), the liberator of the Maldives. ### P104 -- Mohamed Thakurufaanu al-Auzam (Utheemu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Mohamed Thakurufaanu al-Auzam, universally known as Bodu Thakurufaanu ("the Great Thakurufaanu"), is the preeminent national hero of the Maldives. Born on Utheemu island in Haa Alif Atoll around 1520, he was the son of Katheebu Hussain of Utheemu (P103) and Lady Aiminaa Dhiye of Ihavandhoo. His family claimed descent from the Hilaaly dynasty through Kalu Hassan Kaleyge of Baararh, Thiladhummathi Atoll.

Together with his brothers Ali Thakurufaanu (P200) and Hassan Thakurufaanu (P201), Mohamed launched a guerrilla campaign against the Portuguese-backed regent Andiri Andirin who governed the Maldives on behalf of Dom Manoel (P61). The brothers built a fast vessel called the Kalhuoffummi on Minicoy Island and for eight years (1570-1573) conducted daring night raids across the atolls, rallying popular support against the Christian administration.

The liberation campaign culminated in 1573 with a decisive night raid on Male, during which the brothers infiltrated the Portuguese fort and killed the Captain-General Andiri Andirin. Mohamed Thakurufaanu was acclaimed as sultan, establishing the Utheemu dynasty. His full formal style, Ghazi Mohamed Thakurufaanu al-Auzam, combined the Arabic honorific Ghazi ("warrior for the faith") with the superlative al-Auzam ("the Greatest"). He was also the first Maldivian sultan to form a Lashkaru (unified military body).

Upon ascending the throne, Mohamed appointed his brother Hassan Thakurufaanu as Rannabandeyri Kilegefaanu (joint ruler). The Maldivian National Day (Qaumee Dhuvas) commemorates his victory. His legacy extends beyond military liberation to the cultural reassertion of Islamic identity after a period of Christian governance. The French traveler Pyrard de Laval, who visited the Maldives in the early seventeenth century, referred to the liberation hero as "Bode ta Courou" (Bodu Thakuru, meaning "great lord").

**Notes**: Promoted record with person_confidence A. yb:1520, yd:1585. The enrichments bio is thorough and well-sourced. Wikipedia confirms all major details. Minor addition: the guerrilla campaign ran 8 years (1570-1573 per Wikipedia), and Hassan was appointed Rannabandeyri. No corrections to dataset needed.

### P75 -- Hassan X (Isdu)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Hassan X, sometimes known by the sobriquet Addu Hassan Manikfan, succeeded his father Ali VII (P74) immediately upon the latter's death on October 27, 1701. His mother was Aisha Rani Kilege. He married Thubbeegedharu Kilavethi Aisha Manikufa'anu and fathered three sons and one daughter.

His reign was cut short when his cousin Ibrahim Mudzhiruddine (P76) seized the throne. Hassan was captured and banished to Hithadu in Addu Atoll on January 1, 1705, beginning a remarkably long exile. He survived in banishment for over half a century, finally dying at Hithadu in June 1758 -- one of the longest-lived exiled monarchs in Maldivian history.

**Notes**: Base record. No yb/yd in base. re:1701-1701. Son of P74 confirmed. Enrichment bio provides detailed sourced account. The exile duration (1705-1758, over 53 years) is extraordinary but attested. No corrections needed.

### P76 -- Ibrahim Mudzhiruddine (Isdu)

### P79 -- Mohamed Imaduddine III (Dhiyamigili)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Mohamed Imaduddine III suffered one of the most harrowing fates of any Maldivian sovereign. Born on January 14, 1717 at Kabafa'anu Palace in Male, he was the full brother of Ibrahim Iskandar II (P78), sharing the same mother, Amina Kabadi Kilege. He succeeded his brother in February 1750.

His reign was cut brutally short on December 18, 1752, when forces loyal to Ali Raja of Cannanore invaded the Maldives. The sultan was abducted and transported to Kavaratti island in the Laccadives, where he was blinded. He languished in exile on Minicoy island until his death on June 25, 1757.

The Malabar seizure of 1752 plunged the Maldives into its most severe crisis since the Portuguese period.

**Notes**: Base record. yb:1717, yd:1757, re:1750-1757, pd:"Minicoy, Laccadive Islands" -- all confirmed. Son of P77 confirmed (confidence B). Full brother of P78 (same mother Amina Kabadi Kilege). Enrichment bio comprehensive. No corrections needed.

### P81 -- Mohamed Ghiyathuddine (Dhiyamigili)
**Status**: verified
**Bio**:
Mohamed Ghiyathuddine, bearing the Arabic laqab Ghiyath al-Din meaning "Succor of the Faith," was the last sultan of the Dhiyamigili dynasty. Born before October 6, 1733 at Kabafa'anu Palace in Male, he was the son of Ibrahim Iskandar II (P78) and Aisha Kabafa'anu. His popular epithet Haajee Bandaarain reflected his completion of the Hajj pilgrimage.

He came to the throne in 1766 as a Dhiyamigili restoration figure after the Huraagey founder Hassan Izzuddine (P80) died. He married three times and fathered a son, Abdullah Ibrahim (later Prince Ibrahim Faamuladheyri, P130), born in 1760 (though Wikipedia gives April 16, 1766 for P130's birth). Secondary sources credit him with compiling the first known Thaana dictionary.

His reign ended tragically: returning from Hajj in October 1774, he was informed that he had been deposed. Despite being warned, he proceeded to Male waters and was drowned off Guraidu island on October 8, 1774, with weights tied to his body. Some historical narratives remember him as Shaheed Kulha Bandaarain, a martyr ruler, confirming his death was not accidental.

**Notes**: Base record. yb:1733, yd:1774, re:1766-1774. Son of P78 confirmed (confidence B). Parent of P130 confirmed in promoted edges. Known_as "Haajee Bandaarain" and "Muhammadh Manikfaan" confirmed. Enrichment bio comprehensive. Wikipedia adds detail about weights tied to his body and his awareness of the coup before returning. No corrections to dataset needed.

### P131 -- Mohamed Didi (Dhiyamigili-South)
