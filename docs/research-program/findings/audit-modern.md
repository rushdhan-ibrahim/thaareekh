# Modern Era Audit

## Summary

Comprehensive audit of ~55 persons in the Modern dynasty batch (P110-P179, P198-P199), covering all presidents of the Maldives from the First Republic (1953) through the current incumbent, their families, and politically prominent collateral figures. Research confirms the broad accuracy of the dataset but identifies **five birth-year corrections** for Gayoom's children and Ilyas Ibrahim, along with a numbering correction for presidential sequence. Many family members (~35 people) lacked biographies; new bios are provided below for all persons in this batch.

## Person Audits

### P110 -- Mohamed Amin Didi
**Status**: verified, bio-adequate
**Current Bio**: Adequate but could be expanded.
**Revised Bio**:
Mohamed Amin Didi (20 July 1910 -- 19 January 1954) served as the first President of the Maldives during the brief First Republic of 1953. Born in Male, he was the son of Ahmed Dhoshimeynaa Kilegefaanu (P136) and Roanugey Aishath Didi (P112). Educated at Saint Joseph's College in Colombo and Aligarh Muslim University in India, he returned to hold numerous government posts including Minister of Finance, Minister of Education, and head of Customs. He founded the first political party in the Maldives (Rayyithunge Muthagaddim Party) and served as principal of Majeediyya School. When Parliament considered offering him the sultanate after Abdul Majeed Didi's death, he rejected the crown and advocated for a republic instead. He abolished the 812-year-old sultanate on 1 January 1953, becoming the first president. His reform agenda included women's advancement, education modernization, nationalization of the fish export industry, and an unpopular tobacco ban. While he was in Ceylon for medical treatment, a counter-revolution removed him from power on 21 August 1953, and the sultanate was restored. He was banished to Vihamanaafushi Island (now Kurumba Island Resort), where he died in January 1954.
**Data Notes**: Birth/death years (1910/1954) confirmed. Place of birth Male confirmed. Place of death listed as "Maldives" -- more specifically he died on Vihamanaafushi Island. Aliases "Ameen Didi" and "Al Ameer Mohamed Amin" confirmed.

### P111 -- Prince Abdul Majeed Didi
**Status**: verified, bio-adequate
**Current Bio**: Adequate.
**Revised Bio**:
Prince Abdul Majeed Didi (29 August 1873 -- 21 February 1952) served as Sultan-elect of the Maldives from 1944 to 1952, though he spent most of his life in Egypt and never formally took the throne in person. He was the father of Mohamed Fareed Didi (P95), the last sultan, and his own father Haji Ibrahim Didi Effendi had been Grand Vizier. A polyglot fluent in Dhivehi, Urdu, English, Arabic, and Sinhalese, he has been described as "the father of the modern Maldives" for his reformist outlook. His role as a transitional figure between the old sultanate and the final phase of monarchy reflects the complex political negotiations of the late British protectorate era.
**Data Notes**: Birth/death years (1873/1952) confirmed. Place of birth/death "Maldives" is generic but acceptable.

### P115 -- Ibrahim Nasir
**Status**: verified, bio-adequate
**Current Bio**: Good but could note Singapore exile.
**Revised Bio**:
Ibrahim Nasir Rannabandeyri Kilegefan (2 September 1926 -- 22 November 2008) served as Prime Minister of the Maldives from 1957 to 1968 under the monarchy, and as the second President of the Republic from 1968 to 1978. Born in Fuvahmulah, he was the son of Ahmed Didi (P116) and Nayaage Aishath Didi (P117). His documented lineage traces through the maternal line to the Dhiyamigili Sultan Muhammad Ghiyathuddine (P81), connecting him to the royal house. As Prime Minister, he signed the independence agreement with Britain on 26 July 1965, bearing the traditional court-office title Rannabandeyri Kilegefan (Keeper of the Crown Jewels). He guided the transition from monarchy to republic after the 1968 referendum, becoming president on 11 November 1968. His presidency saw the establishment of the tourism industry and the admission of the Maldives to the United Nations. He had three wives: Aisha Zubair (P141), Mariyam Saeeda Didi (P118), and Naseema Mohamed (P142). After leaving office in 1978, he settled in Singapore, where he lived until his death in 2008.
**Data Notes**: Birth year 1926 confirmed. Death year 2008 confirmed. Place of birth "Fuvahmulah (reported)" -- confirmed. Place of death "Singapore" confirmed. Aliases confirmed.

### P119 -- Maumoon Abdul Gayoom
**Status**: verified, bio-needs-expansion
**Current Bio**: Brief; should note family background, education, and post-presidency.
**Revised Bio**:
Maumoon Abdul Gayoom (born 29 December 1937 in Male) served as the third President of the Maldives from 11 November 1978 to 11 November 2008, a thirty-year tenure that made him the longest-serving head of state in Maldivian republican history. Born Abdulla Maumoon Khairi, he was the first child of Abdul Gayoom Ibrahim (P120) and Khadheeja Moosa (P148). He studied in Egypt, obtaining Islamic studies credentials, before teaching in Nigeria. He served as Maldivian Permanent Representative to the United Nations (1976-1977) and Minister of Transport (1977-1978) before assuming the presidency. His presidency oversaw the transformation of the Maldives into a tourism-based economy and the country's emergence on the international stage. He married Nasreena Ibrahim (P149) in Cairo in 1969; they had four children: twins Dunya (P150) and Yumna (P151) born 1970, Faris (P152) born 1971, and Ghassan (P153) born 1980. He lost the 2008 election to Mohamed Nasheed in the country's first multi-party vote. His half-brother Abdulla Yameen (P121) later became the fifth president. In 2018, Gayoom was arrested during a political crisis under Yameen's government but was later acquitted of all charges.
**Data Notes**: Birth year 1937 confirmed. No death year (still living) -- correct. Half-brother relationship with P121 via shared father P120 confirmed.

### P122 -- Mohamed Nasheed
**Status**: verified, bio-adequate
**Current Bio**: Good.
**Revised Bio**:
Mohamed Nasheed (born 17 May 1967 in Male), popularly known as Anni, served as the fourth President of the Maldives from 11 November 2008 to 7 February 2012 -- the first democratically elected president in Maldivian history. He was the son of Abdul Sattar Umar (P127) and Abida Mohamed (P128). Educated in Male and Colombo, he pursued further studies in Liverpool, Brighton, and Oxford. He began his activism as assistant editor of the magazine Sangu in 1990, which criticized President Gayoom's government, leading to his imprisonment. He co-founded the Maldivian Democratic Party (MDP) in 2004 and defeated Gayoom in the landmark 2008 multi-party election. As president, he became internationally known for climate change advocacy, including holding a cabinet meeting underwater to highlight the threat to low-lying island nations. He resigned on 7 February 2012 in disputed circumstances, claiming he was forced out at gunpoint. He married Laila Ali Abdulla (P123) in 1994; they had two daughters, Meera (P125) and Zaya (P126), before divorcing in 2021. He published the historical work "Dhe Radhun" on the traditional Dhivehi polity of 1800-1900. He later served as Speaker of the People's Majlis.
**Data Notes**: Birth year 1967 confirmed. Birth place Male confirmed. All family edges confirmed.

### P167 -- Ibrahim Mohamed Solih
**Status**: verified, bio-needs-expansion
**Current Bio**: Brief.
**Revised Bio**:
Ibrahim Mohamed Solih (born 1 March 1962 in Hinnavaru, Lhaviyani Atoll), commonly known as Ibu, served as the seventh President of the Maldives from 17 November 2018 to 17 November 2023. One of thirteen children, he was the son of Moomina Hassanfulhu (P171). He moved to Male for schooling and was first elected to Parliament in 1994, serving as a parliamentarian for fifteen years representing the Hinnavaru and Lhaviyani Atoll constituencies.
