## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Dynastic succession logic
- Alternate labels/spellings: Succession legitimacy framework; collateral-house transfer logic.
- Language/script forms: Dhivehi chronicle formulations and later English historical summaries.

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: A legitimacy model where succession may move through paternal, maternal, collateral, or affinal corridors depending on political settlement.
- Historical scope and periodization: Early Lunar through Hilaaly/Huraagey transitions, including episodes of split claims and restoration.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Many relationship edges in contested eras are context-kin links rather than strict parentage, and this concept explains that modeling choice.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Graph rule: do not automatically convert a succession claim into biological parent-child unless the source explicitly gives lineage wording.
- When multiple claimants appear in close chronology, preserve competing branch assertions in the contradiction log rather than flattening to one line.
- Use this concept to interpret high-impact inferred bridges such as P31/P32 and southern continuity claims before promotion.
