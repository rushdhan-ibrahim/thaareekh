# Concept Entry

Concept ID: `CONCEPT-011`
Last updated: `2026-02-09`
Category: `style/title`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Bandaarain cluster
- Alternate labels/spellings: Bandaara, Bandeyri, Don Bandaara, Donbandaarain, Haajee Bandaarain, Bandaarain-family style set.
- Language/script forms: Dhivehi court-style forms (ބަނޑާރައިން / ބަނޑެއިރި) with transliteration variation across modern summaries. Possibly derived from Sanskrit/Pali *bhandara* (treasury/storehouse) or a Dhivehi adaptation of a court-function term.

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: A style-title cluster tied to courtly and elite identity signals, encompassing both the office of Bandeyri (palace/household portfolio) and associated honorific sobriquets used by the Huraagey dynasty founders.
- Historical scope and periodization:
  - **Office origin**: The Bandeyri office (OFF-BANDEYRI) is documented in the Furadaana council from at least the early 17th century (Pyrard account), with functions relating to the royal household, palace personnel, and state administration.
  - **Huraagey dynasty usage (1759+)**: The founder Hassan Izzuddine (`P80`) is known as "Dhon Bandaarain" or "Don Bandaara", linking the Bandaarain honorific directly to the Huraagey line. His descendant Mohamed Ghiyathuddine (`P81`) is known as "Haajee Bandaarain".
  - **18th-19th century**: Bandaarain-like tokens appear as known-as or sobriquet forms for Huraagey-era rulers and elite figures.
  - **Modern persistence**: The Bandaarain forms survive in genealogical narratives and secondary source king lists.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Bandaarain-like tokens appear in known-as fields and can be mistaken for strict lineage markers. The title can denote both an office-bearing function and a personal/dynastic sobriquet.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Keep Bandaarain-family terms in style/sobriquet layers unless a source explicitly defines office function for a specific individual.
- When both office and sobriquet uses exist for the same term, annotate both but do not collapse them into a single kin inference rule.
- The Huraagey dynasty's association with the Bandaarain title is well-attested: `P80` (founder) was known as Donbandaarain, likely because his family held the Bandeyri office before ascending to the throne. This provides institutional context for the dynasty's rise.
- The Portuguese-era prefix "Don" in "Don Bandaara" should be cross-referenced with CONCEPT-013 (Dom-Donna-Infanta overlay), as it reflects the Huraagey line's descent from the Goa-linked Hilaaly branch.
- Maintain uncertainty markers for unresolved etymology. The relationship between *Bandeyri* (office) and *Bandaarain* (honorific) needs philological confirmation.
