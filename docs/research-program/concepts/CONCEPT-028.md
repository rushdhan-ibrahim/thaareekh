# Concept Entry

Concept ID: `CONCEPT-028`
Last updated: `2026-02-09`
Category: `title/system`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Arabic laqab / honorific system
- Alternate labels/spellings: *Laqab* (لقب, Arabic honorific title); Arabic regnal epithets; *-uddine* / *-uddin* suffix; Islamic royal naming.
- Language/script forms: Arabic *laqab* (لقب); common Maldivian examples: *Imaduddine* (عماد الدين, "Pillar of the Faith"), *Shamsuddine* (شمس الدين, "Sun of the Faith"), *Nooredine/Nooruddine* (نور الدين, "Light of the Faith"), *Muizzuddine* (معز الدين, "Strengthener of the Faith"), *Ghiyathuddine* (غياث الدين, "Succour of the Faith"), *Izzuddine* (عز الدين, "Glory of the Faith"), *Mohyedine* (محي الدين, "Reviver of the Faith").

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: The Arabic-layer honorific system adopted by Maldivian sultans, consisting of compound Arabic titles (typically *X-uddine*, "X of the Faith") that were appended to or replaced indigenous Dhivehi regnal names, becoming increasingly standard from the Utheemu period onward.
- Historical scope and periodization:
  - **Early adoption (post-1153)**: Arabic-Islamic names were adopted immediately upon conversion. Sultan Muhammad al-Adil (`P2`) is the first attested Islamic name. However, early Lunar dynasty sultans primarily used Indic-origin regnal names (CONCEPT-014) with Arabic personal names.
  - **Hilaaly transitional period (1388-1583)**: Arabic laqab begins to appear more frequently alongside Indic tokens. Some Hilaaly rulers used compound names mixing both traditions.
  - **Utheemu-Dhiyamigili standardization (1573-1766)**: Arabic laqab becomes standard. The Utheemu founders use *Imaduddine* and related forms. The pattern of *Mohamed [Laqab]* or *Ibrahim [Laqab]* becomes formulaic.
  - **Huraagey repetition (1759-1968)**: The Arabic laqab system becomes highly formulaic with repeated use of a small set of epithets (*Imaduddine*, *Shamsuddine*, *Nooredine*, *Muizzuddine*, *Mueenuddine*) across generations, creating disambiguation challenges.
  - **Comparison with broader Islamic world**: The *-uddine* laqab pattern is pan-Islamic, found from Morocco to Indonesia. The Maldivian usage follows the general Islamic convention but with a relatively limited repertoire of epithets compared to larger Islamic states.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: The repeated use of identical Arabic laqab across different rulers (e.g., multiple *Imaduddine*, multiple *Shamsuddine*) creates disambiguation challenges. Laqab alone cannot identify a person; it must be combined with regnal number, dynasty, and chronology.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Key Arabic laqab used by Maldivian sultans and their meanings:
  - *Imaduddine* (عماد الدين): "Pillar of the Faith" -- used by at least 6 sultans including `P68`, `P77`, `P79`, `P86`, `P90`, `P92`
  - *Shamsuddine* (شمس الدين): "Sun of the Faith" -- used by `P72`, `P82`, `P88`, `P91`
  - *Nooredine/Nooruddine* (نور الدين): "Light of the Faith" -- used by `P84`, `P87`, `P94`
  - *Muizzuddine* (معز الدين): "Strengthener of the Faith" -- used by `P83`
  - *Ghiyathuddine* (غياث الدين): "Succour of the Faith" -- used by `P81`
  - *Izzuddine* (عز الدين): "Glory of the Faith" -- used by `P80`
  - *Mohyedine* (محي الدين): "Reviver of the Faith" -- used by `P71`
  - *Mueenuddine* (معين الدين): "Helper of the Faith" -- used by `P85`, `P88`
  - *Shihabuddine* (شهاب الدين): "Star of the Faith" -- used by `P19`
  - *Iskander* (اسكندر): "Alexander" (a kunya/nisba rather than laqab, but used similarly) -- used by `P69`, `P78`
- The laqab does not indicate kinship. Different rulers with the same laqab may be unrelated.
- In the broader Islamic world, laqab was often bestowed by a caliph or religious authority. In the Maldives, it is unclear whether laqab were self-adopted, bestowed by the Fandiyaaru, or inherited.
- Cross-reference with CONCEPT-014 (Regnal epithet token bank) for the Indic-layer naming that the Arabic laqab gradually supplemented and partially replaced.
- Cross-reference with CONCEPT-005 (Islamization naming shifts) for the broader naming transformation context.
