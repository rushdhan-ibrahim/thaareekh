# Concept Entry

Concept ID: `CONCEPT-013`
Last updated: `2026-02-09`
Category: `system/style`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Dom-Donna-Infanta overlay
- Alternate labels/spellings: Iberian Christian honorific layer; Portuguese-contact naming overlay; Goa-linked identity forms.
- Language/script forms: Portuguese honorifics (*Dom*, *Donna*, *Infanta*) used alongside Dhivehi and Islamicate name forms. Also includes Portuguese personal names (*Manoel*, *Joao*, *Philippe*, *Luis*, *Maria*, *Francisca*).

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: A contact-era naming overlay where Iberian honorific and Christian-style names coexist with local dynastic naming, creating identity-matching risk across source traditions.
- Historical scope and periodization:
  - **Portuguese arrival (1558)**: Portuguese forces under Captain Andiri Andirin established control over the Maldives, imposing Christianity and Portuguese naming conventions on converted royals.
  - **Portuguese period (1558-1573)**: Maldivian rulers who converted to Christianity adopted Portuguese names. Hassan IX (`P61`) became Dom Manoel after baptism by St. Francis Xavier.
  - **Goa exile branch (1573-1632+)**: After the liberation of 1573, some members of the royal family remained in Goa and continued using Portuguese names. Joao (`P66`) and Philippe (`P67`) ruled in absentia from Goa.
  - **Huraagey ancestry corridor**: The Huraagey dynasty founder Hassan Izzuddine (`P80`) descends from Joao (`P66`) through the Dom Luis branch, creating a genealogical corridor where Portuguese names persist in ancestry chains even for later Islamic-style rulers.
  - **Historiographic reuse**: Later secondary sources continue to reference Portuguese-form names when discussing this period.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Mixed-form names can split one person into multiple nodes or force incorrect merges if treated as plain aliases. The dual naming system requires careful identity harmonization.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Always pair Portuguese-form names with chronology and relation evidence before identity merging. "Dom Manoel" and "Hassan IX" refer to the same person (`P61`), but this identity equation requires explicit source support.
- Keep alias provenance explicit: which source tradition uses the Iberian form versus local form.
- Treat honorific presence as identity metadata, not kin confirmation.
- The Portuguese period lasted only 15 years (1558-1573) in terms of direct control, but the Goa-linked branch persisted much longer, keeping Portuguese naming conventions alive in the genealogical record.
- The ancestry chain from `P61` (Dom Manoel) through `P66` (Joao) through Dom Luis to `P80` (Hassan Izzuddine) is one of the most important in the graph, as it bridges the Hilaaly and Huraagey dynasties.
- Donna Ines, daughter of `P66`, is the key linking figure between the Goa-branch Hilaaly line and the Huraagey founders. Her Portuguese-form name coexists with an unknown Dhivehi name.
- Cross-reference with CONCEPT-011 (Bandaarain) for the "Don Bandaara" form and its relationship to Portuguese-era naming.
