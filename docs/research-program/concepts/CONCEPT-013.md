## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Dom-Donna-Infanta overlay
- Alternate labels/spellings: Iberian Christian honorific layer; Portuguese-contact naming overlay.
- Language/script forms: Portuguese/Latinized honorifics used alongside Dhivehi and Islamicate name forms.

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: A contact-era naming overlay where Iberian honorific and Christian-style names coexist with local dynastic naming, creating identity-matching risk.
- Historical scope and periodization: Primarily sixteenth-seventeenth century contacts, plus later historiographic reuse.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Mixed-form names can split one person into multiple nodes or force incorrect merges if treated as plain aliases.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Always pair these forms with chronology and relation evidence before identity merging.
- Keep alias provenance explicit: which source tradition uses the Iberian form versus local form.
- Treat honorific presence as identity metadata, not kin confirmation.
