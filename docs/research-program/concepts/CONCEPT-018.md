# Concept Entry

Concept ID: `CONCEPT-018`
Last updated: `2026-02-09`
Category: `institution`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Atoll governance (Atholhu Veriyaa)
- Alternate labels/spellings: Atoll chief; *Atholhuveri* / *Atholhuveriyaa* (އަތޮޅުވެރިޔާ); Atoll administration; island chief (*Katheeb*).
- Language/script forms: Dhivehi *atholhuveriyaa* (atoll chief); *katheeb* (island chief); English "Atoll Chief"; modern Dhivehi administrative terminology.

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: The system of atoll-level governance in the Maldives, where appointed local leaders (Atholhu Veriyaa) managed island affairs under the authority of the central sultanate in Male, serving as the primary mechanism for extending sovereign power across a geographically dispersed archipelago.
- Historical scope and periodization:
  - **Sultanate era (1153-1968)**: Each atoll operated with a degree of autonomy under appointed atoll chiefs who managed tax collection, resource allocation, dispute resolution, and communication with Male. These chiefs reported to the central authority but exercised substantial local power.
  - **Colonial influence (19th-20th century)**: British protectorate (1887-1965) preserved the atoll governance system while adding treaty obligations at the central level.
  - **Late monarchy/early republic**: Administrative control became increasingly centralized in Male under the Ministry of Atoll Administration, which managed appointments of atoll chiefs and island office holders.
  - **Gayoom era (1978-2008)**: Highly centralized administration where the president appointed atoll chiefs through the Ministry of Atoll Administration, tightening Male's control over the atolls.
  - **Decentralization Act (2010)**: Replaced appointed chiefs with elected atoll, island, and city councils, fundamentally transforming atoll governance.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Atoll governance structures explain how royal authority extended beyond Male, why exile to outer atolls was politically significant (CONCEPT-021), and how provincial elite families connected to the central royal lineage.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- The atoll chief (*Atholhuveriyaa*) was the sovereign's representative in each atoll, combining executive, judicial, and fiscal functions at the local level.
- Island-level administration was managed by *katheeb* (island chiefs), subordinate to the atoll chief. The term *katheeb* is likely cognate with the Arabic *katib* (scribe/secretary), suggesting a documentary/administrative origin. See CONCEPT-026 for the Kateeb/scribal office.
- The geographic dispersion of the Maldives (roughly 1,200 islands across 26 atolls stretching 871 km north-to-south) made centralized governance exceptionally difficult and gave atoll chiefs substantial practical autonomy.
- Exile to outer atolls (see CONCEPT-021) was politically effective precisely because the exiled person was placed under the authority of a loyal atoll chief, far from the power center of Male.
- Southern atolls (Addu, Huvadhu, Fuvahmulah) historically had the most independent-minded governance, culminating in the United Suvadive Republic secession of 1959-1963.
