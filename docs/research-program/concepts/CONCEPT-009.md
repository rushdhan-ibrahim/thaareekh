Last updated: `2026-02-08`  
Category: `style/system`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Sovereign style stack
- Alternate labels/spellings: Sultanate-to-presidency style transition layer; crown/executive title stack.
- Language/script forms: Dhivehi regnal forms, Arabic-influenced royal styles, and modern English republican office labels.

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: A periodized style system where top-office language shifts from monarchical forms (`Sultan`, `Queen`) to republican executive form (`President`) while preserving genealogical continuity corridors.
- Historical scope and periodization: Monarchical period through the 1968 constitutional shift and the post-1968 presidency system.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Title terms can imply political rank, but they do not by themselves resolve parentage/sibling certainty.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Treat sovereign style as office-state metadata, not automatic kinship evidence.
- During transition eras, preserve both old and new office language where sources mix terminology.
- Promotion from inferred to confirmed must still rely on direct pairwise kin wording even when both nodes are sovereign office holders.
