# Concept Entry

Concept ID: `CONCEPT-009`
Last updated: `2026-02-09`
Category: `system/style`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Sovereign style stack
- Alternate labels/spellings: Sultanate-to-presidency style transition layer; crown/executive title stack; Al-Sultan / Sultana / President layering.
- Language/script forms: Dhivehi regnal forms (e.g. *Kula Sundhura Katthiri Bavana*), Arabic-influenced royal styles (*Al-Sultan*, *Sultana*), Portuguese-contact royal forms (*Dom*, *Rei*), and modern English republican office labels (*President*).

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: A periodized style system where top-office language shifts from monarchical forms (Sultan, Sultana/Queen) to republican executive form (President) while preserving genealogical continuity corridors between officeholders.
- Historical scope and periodization:
  - **Pre-Islamic**: Buddhist-era royal styles (Maha Radun, Bavana-family epithets) associated with the Solar and Lunar dynasty traditions.
  - **Early Islamic (1153-1388)**: Lunar dynasty sultans adopt Arabic-Islamic regnal styles alongside indigenous Dhivehi epithets. Female sovereign style appears with Sultana Khadijah (`P20`, r. 1347-1380).
  - **Hilaaly period (1388-1573/1609)**: Continuation of dual-layer naming; Portuguese-contact era introduces Dom/Donna overlay for converted rulers (e.g. `P61` as Dom Manoel).
  - **Utheemu-Dhiyamigili-Huraagey (1573-1968)**: Consolidated Islamic sultanic style with Arabic laqab becoming standard (Imaduddine, Shamsuddine, Nooredine, etc.).
  - **First Republic (1953-1954)**: Brief presidential experiment under Amin Didi (`P110`).
  - **Restored Sultanate (1954-1968)**: Last sultan Mohamed Farid (`P95`).
  - **Second Republic (1968-present)**: Presidential office replaces sultanate; style shifts to executive/constitutional language.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: Title terms (Sultan, President) can imply political rank, but they do not by themselves resolve parentage or sibling certainty. Confusing office-style continuity with blood-line continuity inflates edge confidence.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- Treat sovereign style as office-state metadata, not automatic kinship evidence. A person holding the title "Sultan" is not necessarily the biological son of the previous Sultan.
- During transition eras (e.g. 1953-1954 republic, 1968 republic), preserve both old and new office language where sources mix terminology.
- The 1932 Constitution codified succession requirements: male, Sunni Muslim, aged 17+, literate in Dhivehi, descended from the Huraagey line of Ghazi Hassan Izzuddine (`P80`). This formalized what had been customary.
- Promotion from inferred to confirmed must still rely on direct pairwise kin wording even when both nodes are sovereign office holders.
- Female sovereign style (*Sultana*, *Rehendhi*) is rare but historically attested: `P20` (Khadijah), `P25` (Raadhaafathi), `P27` (Dhaain) all held the throne. See CONCEPT-022 for detailed treatment.
- The Portuguese period introduced a parallel Christian royal style for Hilaaly rulers in Goa (`P61`, `P66`, `P67`), creating a dual-register naming system. See CONCEPT-013 for Dom-Donna overlay.
