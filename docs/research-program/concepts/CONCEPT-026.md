# Concept Entry

Concept ID: `CONCEPT-026`
Last updated: `2026-02-09`
Category: `office/institution`

## 1) Canonical label
- Primary label: Kateeb (chancery/scribal) office
- Alternate labels/spellings: *Katheeb* (ކާތިބު); *Katib* (Arabic, scribe/secretary); island chief; scribal tradition; chancery record-keeping.
- Language/script forms: Dhivehi *katheeb* (island chief/scribe, from Arabic *katib* كاتب); associated with Thaana script development and the broader scribal tradition.

## 2) Definition
- Short definition: The scribal-administrative office in the Maldivian governance system, responsible for record-keeping, correspondence, and documentary administration, whose practices shaped the survival of historical sources and whose island-level function (*katheeb* as island chief) connected local governance to the central sultanate.
- Historical scope and periodization:
  - **Pre-Thaana period**: Record-keeping in earlier scripts (Dhivehi Akuru, Eveyla Akuru) was maintained by literate officials. The scribal function predates the Thaana script.
  - **Thaana script development (c. 1599-1703)**: The Thaana script first appeared in a Maldivian inscription around 1599 in a crude form known as *Gabulhi* ('incomplete') Thaana. The script was originally used for *fandita* (magical) incantations by learned men versed in sorcery. It gradually evolved for everyday administrative use and appeared in government documents by 1703.
  - **Thaana design**: The alphabet consists of 24 letters, the first 9 derived from Arabic numerals (1-9) and the next 9 from old Dhivehi numerals. This unique origin reflects a deliberate encoding of numerical knowledge into a writing system.
  - **Island-level administration**: The *katheeb* at the island level served as both chief and record-keeper, subordinate to the atoll chief (CONCEPT-018). The scribal origin of the title (*katib* = scribe) reveals the documentary basis of local governance.
  - **Tarikh tradition**: The Fandiyaaru (CONCEPT-010) held charge over the recording of the *tarikh* (state chronicle), making the judicial office the primary custodian of scribal tradition at the central level.
  - **Modern literacy**: Thaana became the standard script for Dhivehi, and modern governance continues the documentary tradition through state records.
- Why it matters in this genealogy graph: The scribal tradition determines what sources survive and how they were recorded. Understanding who kept records (Fandiyaaru, katheeb, court scribes) helps assess source bias and completeness.

## 3) Semantic and historical notes
- The *katheeb* title at the island level combines executive (chief) and documentary (scribe) functions. This dual role means that local records were produced by persons with both administrative authority and documentary expertise.
- The Thaana script's origins in magical incantation (*fandita*) practice means that early Thaana texts may have been deliberately obscure or coded, affecting their interpretation as historical sources.
- The transition from Dhivehi Akuru to Thaana script (c. 1599-1703) coincides with the Utheemu and post-Utheemu periods. Documents written in Dhivehi Akuru (the earlier script) are rarer and require specialized paleographic skills.
- The Loamaafaanu copper-plate grants represent the most important surviving documentary evidence from the pre-Thaana scribal tradition.
- Source survival is heavily biased toward documents produced by the central scribal apparatus in Male. Provincial and island-level records are much rarer.
- Cross-reference with CONCEPT-010 (Fandiyaaru) for the judicial office's role in chronicle-keeping, and CONCEPT-018 (Atoll governance) for the katheeb's administrative role.

## 5) Evidence
- The Loamaafaanu copper plates (edited by Hassan Ahmed Maniku, 1982) provide primary documentary evidence.
- The Tarikh and other chronicle manuscripts represent the central scribal tradition's output.
- Thaana script history is documented in linguistic and paleographic studies.
- The National Centre for Linguistic and Historical Research (Maldives) has published on script development.
